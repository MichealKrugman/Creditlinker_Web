Nigeria has over 39 million SMEs contributing 48% of GDP, yet formal credit penetration remains below 5%. The data infrastructure to serve them does not yet exist at scale.

The Data Already Exists

Nigerian businesses transact across dozens of banks and payment platforms every day. The raw signal is rich. It simply is not connected, structured, or made lender-readable. That is the problem Creditlinker solves.

Creditlinker
Business Model

Multiple revenue
streams. One
network effect.

Revenue Stream 01
Business Subscriptions
Businesses pay a monthly or annual fee to maintain their Creditlinker ID, access their identity dashboard, and share their profile with capital providers on the network.
Revenue Stream 02
Institutional API Access
Lenders, fintechs, and institutions pay for API access to financial identity data, verification checks, and risk signals, priced per call or via enterprise licence.
Revenue Stream 03
Pulse Card Interchange
The Creditlinker Pulse Card generates interchange revenue on every transaction, with unit economics that improve as more creditworthy businesses come onto the platform.
Revenue Stream 04
Marketplace and Referral
Creditlinker connects verified businesses directly to matched capital providers, generating referral fees and commissions on successful financing introductions.
Defensibility
Every business that builds a Creditlinker ID adds to a proprietary dataset that improves scoring accuracy and deepens the platform's value for institutions. The network gets stronger with every new participant.
